Towed Vehicle Fast Response

This page is built for “panic mode” — do this in order, fast, and don’t argue on scene. If you need us to step in, submit the lead form after you stabilize the situation.

First rule: recover the car, then fight the fees. Missing deadlines and storage escalation is what breaks people.

Step 1 — Confirm the tow + location

  • Call the non-emergency police line / 311 (or the city tow desk) and confirm: tow yard name, address, phone, tow reason.
  • Ask if a hold exists (police hold / investigation) — if yes, ask who releases it and how.

Step 2 — Stop storage from exploding

  • Ask the tow yard: daily storage rate, gate fee, payment methods, and required documents.
  • If you can’t pick up today, ask for hours and the earliest pickup slot.

Step 3 — Bring the right documents

  • Driver’s license
  • Registration / title (or proof of ownership)
  • Insurance card (if needed)
  • If not registered to you: notarized letter / power of attorney (varies by yard)

Step 4 — Document everything (quietly)

  • Take photos/video: tow signs, where vehicle was parked, street signs, meter/signage, damage, receipts.
  • Get a copy of: tow receipt, inventory sheet, release form, and payment receipt.

Step 5 — Get the car first

  • Pay under protest if needed (keep all receipts).
  • Do not “confess” verbally. Keep it clean: “I’m retrieving my vehicle. I’ll address disputes through proper process.”

Step 6 — Dispute path (after recovery)

  • Identify the towing authority (city agency / private lot / property manager).
  • Request the tow authorization record (ticket, tow order, photos, logs).
  • File the formal dispute/appeal per your city’s process.
